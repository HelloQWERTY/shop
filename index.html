Life In An Ecosystem

Written by John Doe, June 20, 2018
Ecosystem Environment

Ecosystems are the circle of life, the interdependency of both living organisms and non-living materials. Living organisms include both big and small, such as the animals, plants, bacteria, and micro-organisms. Non-living organisms, such as the soil or the sun, hold important roles in keeping a balanced ecosystem. A healthy ecosystem begins begins with the plants consuming sunlight, and creating oxygen for the surrounding life forms. Herbivores such as deer consume the plants for nutrients allowing them to grow, but are prey to organisms at the top of the food chain.

Written by John Doe, June 26, 2018
Biodiversity

A good example of a healthy ecosystem is the biodiversity or variety of organisms that inhabit an area. According to Gale Student Resources and Context, biologists have discovered over 1.7 million different discrete species of plants and animals around the world today. Life on Earth has shifted greatly throughout its history, increasing and immediate extinction. One example is the Permian-Triassic Extinct Event, when the biological cycle was disturbed, building carbon dioxide in the atmosphere. The ash from the volcanic eruptions also contributed to the lack of sunlight, killing the cycle and the source of food for all animals. As a result, 96% of marine species were extinct and 70% of terrestrial species were extinct.
